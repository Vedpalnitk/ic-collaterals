The thesis

Banks have been adding agents to processes. The next decade is about redesigning processes around agents.

Three things to walk away with

  1. The pilot plateau is a design problem, not a model problem. Better models will not break the ceiling. Redesign will.
  2. Value lives in process redesign and operating-model change — controls, risk, talent — not in better assistants.
  3. The next 24 months will separate banks that "use AI" from banks that are agent-native. Stage three is decided inside this window.

Five no-regret moves to start now

  • i
    Establish an agent control plane.

    Pick one orchestration stack. Standardize. Multiple stacks fragment evals, controls, and talent.

  • ii
    Build the eval discipline.

    Every capability ships with a measurable scorecard. No eval, no promotion. Evals are the only durable asset when models change.

  • iii
    Pick a redesign, not a copilot.

    Choose a process worth redrawing — not just augmenting. Copilots have known ceilings; redesigns don't.

  • iv
    Get the governance ready early.

    Model risk, audit trail, regulator-readable explanations from day one. First-movers shape the precedent.

  • v
    Reshape the talent contract.

    Hire and train agent designers, agent-ops, and agent product managers. The role gap closes in six to twelve months — three years if you wait.
