Infosys Consulting · Design Reference

A working style guide

The IC design system.

Palette, three font families, named visual components, layout patterns, constraints. Every collateral in this folder should be reproducible from what you find here. If a request can't be served by the system as described, that's a gap — surface it.

§ 01 · Constraints

Hard rules — non-negotiable.

These rules must hold from the first turn, before any file is read.

Four things that cannot vary.

  • No burnt orange. IC is blue-led. Warm accents are not used anywhere.
  • No pure black (#000000). Use PAVEMENT #404040 for every "black" text, line, and outline.
  • Deep Blue is used ONLY in slide title bands and section separators. Not column headers, table headers, swim-lanes, callout fills, or chart series. The cover masthead/colophon is a controlled exception.
  • BLUE is the working accent. Use it freely across slides for section heads, accent strokes, chart primary series, callouts, hero figures. LT_BLUE is its softer companion.

§ 02 · Color

Brand palette.

Seven core colors plus three derived tints. Every IC artifact is built from this set; nothing else is allowed.

§ 03 · Color

Tints & rules.

Soft fills for cards and callouts; hairline rules for borders. Use sparingly.

§ 04 · Color

Status & RACI.

Status colors live outside the brand palette so they can be tuned for legibility without dragging the brand around. Pair each solid with its _BG tint for the pill fill.

§ 05 · Typography

Three font families.

Pick the family by the artifact type, not by personal preference. SYSTEM for editable corporate files; EDITORIAL for designed publications; TECHNICAL for engineering documentation. Fraunces and JetBrains Mono are shared between Editorial and Technical — the two families read as siblings.

§ 07 · Layout

Layout patterns.

Two HTML layout patterns have stabilized. Pick by reading mode, not by topic.

Pattern 01

Single-column reading

Centered column (max ~660–720px), generous side margins. Best for long-form prose read top-to-bottom — POV primers, one-pagers, sales narratives. Optional left gutter for chapter markers and sidenotes.

Pattern 02

Sidebar + content

Sticky 280px sidebar TOC + content column. Active section tracked via IntersectionObserver. Best for reference and learning material — engineering notes, technical docs, anything meant to be jumped around in.

§ 08 · Layout

Spacing & dimensions.

A 4-pt base scale. All component spacing snaps to multiples of 4.

§ 09 · Practice

Lessons learned.

Mistakes made in earlier IC collaterals, corrected, documented here so the next artifact doesn't repeat them.

i
Don't double up eyebrows.

If the title-band right side already shows "Reframe · Our point of view," don't add a standalone "OUR POINT OF VIEW" eyebrow underneath. Pick one location for each piece of metadata.

ii
Don't use TINT_BLUE_BG as a full-width band.

It reads as a banner and fights the hairline aesthetic. Use it as a small left-rule strip or inside a contained note/callout box instead.

iii
Don't break titles mid-clause with italic emphasis.

A title like The memo is generated, not authored. reads as broken when it wraps. Make the italic portion a complete phrase: The memo is generated, not authored. followed by a normal continuation.

iv
Don't position rules near large Fraunces text by coordinates.

Fraunces at large sizes has substantial descenders. A BLUE accent rule placed 0.3in below the title may strike through the bottom of letters like 'p', 'g', 'y'. Pad generously, or omit the rule.

v
Don't anchor chart labels to individual bar heights.

When some bars are very tall (40%) and some very short (2%), per-bar label positioning makes the labels float at inconsistent heights. Use a shared label-row Y above the chart so all percentage labels sit together.

vi
Don't put an <a> around all grid children inside an <li>.

A grid defined on the list item only applies to its direct children — the anchor collapses into the first column, and its inner spans wrap one word per line. Either move the grid to the anchor, or make the spans direct children of the list item.
